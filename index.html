More Than Meets The Eye

"I believe design is more than just the shell of an object, more than just manipulation of aesthetics. It's deeper than that. Design is the power to change someone's mood in an instant, inform people without ever talking them, and build things nobody has ever seen before. What I do, as a designer, is take this more-than-meets-the-eye mentality and apply it to industrial products. I strive to bring logic, efficiency, and clean, readable forms to all of my designs." - Nick Holland

Meet Nick

Nick Holland is an undergraduate student in Industrial Design at Iowa State University. His focus is on advanced model making and prototyping with an aim towards clean and sustainable design. When he’s not in class, he teaches both the introduction drawing studio and the introduction design studio as a teacher’s assistant. In his spare time, he practices glassblowing as a trained gaffer at the Iowa State University Gaffer’s Guild.

Top Skills

Product design, graphic design, Adobe Creative Suite, sketching, drawing, photography, marker rendering, CAD Rendering (Solidworks & Keyshot), rapid prototyping, 3D modeling, woodworking, laser cutting, foam modeling, web design, 3D printing, material identification, glassblowing and more.

A Different Perspective

In the Summer of 2013, Nick traveled around Europe and studied abroad for a semester in Rome. While in Rome, he became fully immersed in European culture, literally living in an Italian apartment and working in an Italian design studio for several months. While there, he learned the importance of minimal living and minimal design in modern culture.

Whereabouts

Nick currently spends the majority of his time in Ames, Iowa studying Industrial Design at Iowa State University. During breaks, he travels back to his family home in Springboro, Ohio. He likes to travel whenever possible. Nick currently works as an undergraduate research assistant for Iowa State University's Industrial Design Program.
